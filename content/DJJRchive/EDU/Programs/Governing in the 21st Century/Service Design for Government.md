# Service Design for Government
##### Description
Effective governance isn’t just about policy; it’s about how services are designed, delivered, and experienced. This course equips government professionals with the tools to think like service designers—without becoming designers themselves.

Students will learn how frontline service design shapes government success, how to incorporate design logics into policy and implementation, and how to collaborate effectively with design professionals. The course blends conceptual frameworks, hands-on methods, and implementation tactics to ensure students develop practical skills for improving government services.

A key component of the course is a real-world project, where students work with a government agency to diagnose service challenges, apply design sensibilities, and develop actionable interventions. Through service journey mapping, rapid prototyping, and stakeholder engagement, students will build an interdisciplinary toolkit for making public services more effective, accessible, and citizen-centered.
##### Core Ideas
- Why Service Design Matters in Government: The role of well-designed services in trust, legitimacy, and efficiency.
- Principles of Service Design: Human-centered design, co-creation, iterative problem-solving, and prototyping.
- Government as a Learning System: Feedback loops, adaptive policy, and user-centered policymaking.
- Collaboration Between Policymakers and Designers: How non-designers can effectively work with service designers and integrate their insights into governance structures.

##### Outline
I. **Foundations of Service Design in Government
1. Why Service Design Matters for Policy & Implementation
2. How Government Services Are Experienced by Users (mapping real agency service pain points)
3. Key Service Design Concepts for Non-Designers (human-centered design, iteration, and usability)
4. Understanding Bureaucratic and Institutional Constraints (why design solutions must work within governance realities)
5. Project Kickoff: Agency Briefing & Problem Framing
	- Partner agency presents a real-world service challenge.
	- Students conduct initial research to define the problem space.
 **II. Hands-On Design Sensibilities
6. Field Research & Stakeholder Interviews 
7. Service Journey Mapping Exercise (students analyze the service from multiple stakeholder perspectives)
8. Rapid Prototyping & Iteration Workshop (testing ideas quickly with low-fidelity methods)
9. Midpoint Check-In with Agency Partners (feedback loop on early insights and prototypes)
10. Field Testing
**III. Implementation & Integration**
1. Developing Feasible, Policy-Aligned Design Interventions
    - Translating service design insights into operational improvements.
    - Identifying institutional barriers and workarounds.
2. Scaling and Embedding Change
    - How agencies can adopt and sustain service improvements.
3. Final Presentation & Deliverable
    - Students present findings/recommendations to agency partners.
    - Final product: a set of actionable service design interventions with implementation strategies.
