# Maxims and Mantras
## Pros and Cons of the Pithy Axioms of Public and Private Leadership
#### Description
In this course we use familiar maxims and mantras of leadership as entry points into deeper analysis of management thinking and decision-making. While pithy sayings often serve as shortcuts around careful analysis or rigorous argument, they do contain kernels of accumulated wisdom about leadership, strategy, or organizational behavior. We will examine the origins, evolution, and use of these casual axioms as a way to critically engage with the principles they encode. Our goal is to become practitioners who can engage with the complex realities that maxims (over)simplify while recognizing the value of distilled knowledge, professionals who can unpack and critically examine received wisdom, extract genuine insights from accumulated experience, and who can communicate in ways that motivate and align.
Students acquire skills that link critical thinking and rhetorical work for their leadership and managerial repertoire.

##### Leadership Philosophy & Authority:
"The buck stops here" - Ultimate responsibility and accountability
"Move fast and break things" - Disruption and innovation mindset
"Management by walking around" - Direct engagement and observation
"Personnel is policy" - Power through organizational control

##### Crisis & Risk Management:
"Never waste/let a good crisis go to waste" - Strategic opportunism
"Better safe than sorry" - Precautionary principle
"Too big to fail" - Systemic risk doctrine
"Get ahead of the story" - Proactive crisis response
"No one ever got fired for buying IBM" - Risk aversion justification

##### Business Strategy & Culture:
"[[Culture eats strategy for breakfast]]" - Organizational dynamics
"The customer is always right" - Service philosophy
"Fail fast, fail often" - Innovation approach
"What gets measured gets managed" - Metrics-driven management
"Hire slow, fire fast" - Personnel management philosophy

##### Government & Public Policy:
"Trust but verify" - Diplomatic caution
"Speak softly and carry a big stick" - Power projection
"Government of the people, by the people, for the people" - Democratic ideals
"If you see something, say something" - Citizen surveillance
"In God we trust, all others bring data" - Evidence-based policy

##### Economic/Social Progress:
"[[A rising tide raises all boats]]" - JFK's argument for broad-based growth
"All politics is local" - Tip O'Neill's insight about constituent concerns
"When Main Street prospers, Wall Street prospers" - Economic interconnection
"The business of business is business" - Milton Friedman's shareholder focus, often used to argue both for and against corporate social responsibility

##### Environmental/Systems:
"Think globally, act locally" - Environmental action principle
"We do not inherit the Earth from our ancestors; we borrow it from our children" - Native American proverb adapted for environmentalism
"The tragedy of the commons" - Garrett Hardin's phrase about collective resource management

##### Social Equity:
"A chain is only as strong as its weakest link" - Systems thinking applied to social policy
"Justice delayed is justice denied" - MLK and others on urgency of reform
"An ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure" - Public health principle that expanded to other domains
