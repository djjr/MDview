#### Outline

##### Module 1: Foundations of Regulatory Infrastructure

 - What is regulatory infrastructure? (Norms, laws, institutions, social control, trust, expectations)

 - How regulation enables markets, commerce, and the division of labor

 - The balance between over-regulation, under-regulation, and adaptive regulation

 - Comparative perspective: The role of the state in different economic models (e.g., U.S., EU, Singapore, China)

  

##### Module 2: Financial Markets

 - Why financial markets need regulation (stability, trust, information asymmetry)

 - Key institutions (central banks, securities regulators, banking oversight)

 - Case studies:
	 - U.S. post-2008 financial reforms vs. EU banking regulation
	 - Shadow banking in China vs. traditional banking in the West
	 - The role of financial tech (FinTech) and its regulatory challenges
##### Module 3: Labor Markets

 - Labor law as regulatory infrastructure (contracts, protections, flexibility vs. rigidity)

 - Minimum wage, worker protections, and the gig economy

 - Comparative cases:
	 - Nordic labor models vs. U.S. at-will employment
	 - Germany's co-determination system vs. labor deregulation in emerging economies

##### Module 4: Technology & Digital Governance

 - Regulating innovation: enabling progress while preventing harm

 - AI, data privacy, cybersecurity, and platform governance

 - Comparative cases:
	 - GDPR (EU) vs. U.S. sectoral approach to data privacy
	 - China's AI governance vs. decentralized U.S. regulatory landscape
	 - Open banking regulation in the UK vs. U.S. financial data fragmentation

##### Module 5: Environmental Regulation & Climate Policy

 - The role of government in internalizing externalities (pollution, emissions, resource use)

 - Market-based vs. command-and-control approaches

 - Comparative cases:
	 - EU's carbon pricing vs. U.S. state-level policies
	 - Water regulation in Australia vs. California
	 - Green industrial policy in China vs. deregulation-led growth models

##### Module 6: Housing & Land Use

 - The regulatory determinants of housing affordability (zoning, permitting, rent control)

 - Property rights, urban planning, and supply constraints

 - Comparative cases:
	 - Tokyo's deregulated housing market vs. San Francisco's zoning restrictions
	 - Vienna's public housing model vs. market-driven housing in the U.S.
	 - Singapore's housing policies and public-private partnerships

##### Module 7: Small Business & Entrepreneurship

 - How regulation shapes small business formation, growth, and competition

 - Licensing, taxation, access to credit, and legal structures

 - Comparative cases:
	 - Ease of doing business: U.S. vs. New Zealand vs. India
	 - Gig economy regulation: California's AB5 vs. UK's approach
	 - Informal economies and regulatory burdens in developing nations

##### Module 8: The Future of Regulatory Infrastructure

 - Regulatory agility: How governments can adapt to technological and economic change

 - Experimental approaches (sandboxes, outcome-based regulation, regulatory tech)

 - Lessons from comparative governance: What works, what doesn't, and why?
