# Finding Out - Research Methods for the Practical Policy Activist
#### Description
- **Interpret and apply statistical evidence** to evaluate policy effectiveness, causal inference, and trends.
- **Select appropriate methods** (e.g., regression, significance testing) for different types of policy questions.
- **Communicate uncertainty and findings** clearly to non-technical stakeholders.

Research method is the epistemological core of all science. We believe what you found because you demonstrate that you used accepted techniques to get the world to reveal itself. Learning and using methods is fundamental to professional practice in which some version of the disinterested true is expected to prevail against a motivated false.

In governance and policy, methodological rigor underpins not just what we know, but how we justify action and make decisions under uncertainty. The future government professional must understand method not just as a tool for inquiry, but as a foundation for credibility, legitimacy, and accountability in the work of governance.

Different methods legitimate knowledge in different disciplinary communities, and different methods yield insights on different problems. Methodological choices are not just technical, but reflect deeper epistemic commitments, disciplinary traditions, and practical constraints.

In this course, the future government professional learns how to carry out, how to understand, and how to assess a variety of methods of "finding out." We focus on developing judgment about method selection and trade-offs, understanding the strengths and weaknesses of different approaches, and cultivating respect for the role of method in producing reliable insights.

- For some methods, we learn what they are, when they are indicated or contraindicated, and how they shape the knowledge produced.
- For others, we learn how they work and with what caveats their findings should be consumed.
- For still others, we learn how to implement them rigorously ourselves.

At the end of the course, students will have a grounded respect for the importance of method, a repertoire of methods with which they can engage, and a set of methods that become a part of how they learn about the world in their own work. They will also develop the ability to critically navigate expertise, engage with contested facts, and make reasoned decisions in the face of complexity and uncertainty.
