## LICENSING DESIGN MENU: Key Decisions for AI Safety IVOs

### **ENTRY: What must organizations demonstrate to get licensed?**

**Organizational Basics**

- [ ] Organizational structure, ownership, legal status
- [ ] Independence & conflict of interest policies
- [ ] Quality management system (ISO certified or equivalent)
- [ ] Financial stability & liability insurance

**Expertise & Capacity**

- [ ] Qualified personnel with specific credentials
- [ ] Technical competence in AI safety domains
- [ ] Testing/evaluation facilities and equipment
- [ ] Track record or demonstrated prior experience

**Operational Readiness**

- [ ] Published methodology/procedures for assessments
- [ ] IT systems for reporting and data management
- [ ] Client list and scope of proposed work
- [ ] Fee structure and service commitments

---

### **PROCESS: How do organizations get licensed?**

**Application & Review**

- [ ] Detailed application with supporting documentation
- [ ] Application fee (covers processing costs)
- [ ] Regulatory staff review against published criteria
- [ ] Technical expert evaluation panel

**Assessment**

- [ ] On-site facility inspection/audit
- [ ] Demonstration of assessment procedures
- [ ] Witness evaluation of sample work
- [ ] Background checks on personnel

**Authorization**

- [ ] Defined scope (what systems, what evaluations)
- [ ] License validity period (annual, multi-year)
- [ ] Unique ID number & public registry listing 
- [ ] Initial vs. ongoing fee structure

---

### **OVERSIGHT: How is performance monitored?**

**Regular Monitoring**

- [ ] Periodic inspections (annual, every 2-3 years)
- [ ] Unannounced audits 
- [ ] Real-time data reporting to central database 
- [ ] Annual activity reports required 

**Performance Verification**

- [ ] Covert audits (known-test cases submitted)
- [ ] Statistical analysis of outcomes (anomaly detection)
- [ ] Re-assessment of technical competence 
- [ ] Stakeholder feedback mechanisms

**Ongoing Requirements**

- [ ] Staff qualification maintenance
- [ ] Incident and non-conformity reporting
- [ ] Notification of significant changes
- [ ] Continuing education/training 

---

### **ENFORCEMENT: What happens when things go wrong?**

**Progressive Discipline**

- [ ] Warnings and corrective action plans 
- [ ] Monetary fines (scaled by severity) 
- [ ] Scope restrictions or enhanced monitoring 
- [ ] Temporary suspension

**Serious Violations**

- [ ] License revocation 
- [ ] Individual sanctions (bar personnel)
- [ ] Public disclosure of violations 
- [ ] Referral for criminal prosecution 

**Accountability Mechanisms**

- [ ] Published inspection findings 
- [ ] Performance scorecards/ratings 
- [ ] Civil liability for harms caused 
- [ ] Appeals and grievance process 

---

### **STRUCTURAL CHOICES: How is the system organized?**

**Market Structure**

- [ ] AI developers choose their IVO (competitive market) 
- [ ] Geographic/sectoral franchising 
- [ ] Capacity limits on number of IVOs
- [ ] Government monopoly vs. private licensees 

**Coordination**

- [ ] International mutual recognition agreements 
- [ ] Harmonized standards across jurisdictions
- [ ] Multi-layer oversight (national + international) 
- [ ] Industry self-regulatory organization role 

**Flexibility**

- [ ] Pilot programs before full mandates 
- [ ] Sunset clauses requiring periodic review 
- [ ] Different tiers for different risk levels
- [ ] Grandfathering existing organizations 

---

### **PRIORITIZATION QUESTIONS**

_Use these to guide your selections:_

1. **What matters most for credibility?**
    - Technical expertise, independence, or track record?
2. **What's feasible to verify?**
    - What can regulators actually check vs. must trust?
3. **Where are the biggest risks?**
    - Incompetence, conflicts of interest, or regulatory capture?
4. **What resources are available?**
    - Can we do annual audits or only periodic reviews?
5. **How mature is the field?**
    - Detailed procedures now or flexibility to evolve?
6. **What's the political constraint?**
    - Industry buy-in, international coordination, or legislative authority?

---
_Each checked item adds cost, complexity, and oversight burden—but also quality assurance and public confidence. The art is selecting the right combination for AI safety's unique challenges._
