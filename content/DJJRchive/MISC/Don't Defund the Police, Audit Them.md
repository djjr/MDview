2025-02-27 One thing that shows up a little in the press coverage of the "migrant crackdown" is how ICE, et al. are frequently bringing a lot of fire power to bear on inconsequential targets.

We were promised that the priority would be gangs and drug lords but those are challenging targets.  These were sometimes characterized as "low hanging fruit" which seemed to mean "people who obviously ought to be deported."

But it seems that ICE, DEA, FBI, etc. are frequently bringing big guns to the apprehension of easy-to-grab, unproblematic individuals with visa problems.  One wonders if this suggests that the fat, the wasteful spending, the incompetence, the inability to deliver is more common in agencies like ATF, DEA, and FBI than in "the bureaucracy." Are they choosing these easy targets because, despite all their bluster, all their manpower, all their weapons and gear, they just are not all that effective when it comes to apprehending challenging targets.

Let's start auditing the dollar for dollar efficiency of teams that are outfitted with expensive gear and lots of overtime and need many lawyers to clean up the messes they make after spending months and months getting results which vary wildly in quality in terms of being prosecutable. Let's ask how much it costs to catch one drug selling gang member, etc. How much does it cost to put one really bad guy behind bars or to deport one really bad guy.  And really bad buy is a simple thing to define: someone whose absence from the streets benefits the general welfare in some way that's conceivably measurable.

I bet we'd find that the cost-benefit analysis only works out when you put an arbitrarily high bounty on "bad guy."
