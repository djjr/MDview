#TermsFlipper 2017
What if TOS were negotiated by users rather than dictated by providers?  A proposal from a few years back: TermsFlipper. It's an agent that lives on your device and knows what conditions you'll agree to when downloading apps, what information you are willing to share on what terms. When you and the provider don't see eye to eye, it negotiates a price for meeting YOUR terms or the price it can pay you to meet ITS terms. If the price is unacceptable, it deletes the app.

Users don't waste time reading inpenetrable terms of service. Users don't agree to things they don't actually agree to. And providers get a lot of data about the value users place on different conditions.

Third parties can develop suites of term preferences and negotiation directives; perhaps I use the AARP's "Retiree's Terms for Health Apps" or "AAA Terms for Travel Apps."
