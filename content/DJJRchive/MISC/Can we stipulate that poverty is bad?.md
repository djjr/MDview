\[2025-01-17\] I open an email from Yale today and see this headline: "[Study Looks at Association Between School Segregation and Late-life Dementia](https://ysph.yale.edu/news-article/study-looks-at-association-between-school-segregation-and-dementia)."  Looks like solid research by a scientist who "is exploring long-term strategies to counter the rising rate of dementia."

I found myself wondering about a ratio.  How does the number of big brains whose life work involves adding datasets to the poverty-inequality-and-discrimination-lead-to-bad-outcomes dossier compare to the number of big brains working to change the way things actually work?  

Sometimes it seems like an awful lot of social science is done with some sort of blind faith that findings passively accumulate in the armories of people who are actually doing something about the causal conditions we identify.

Related, perhaps, to the fetishization of [[Data driven decision making]].
